About Me

I've been on the Web since the dawn of time. Well almost. And no, I don't believe I invented it. In the early 90's I worked as an EMT in a hospital Emergency room in Portland where we retrieved medical records from OHSU via something called the Internet —this was before the World Wide Web was readily available through Web browers. When it was slow at night, my co-workers and I would chat on Telnet bulletin boards with university students around Europe. Around the time I found out about the Web, the hospital I worked in closed so I decided to learn HTML. Soon I was making webpages and learning about servers but unforunately there wasn't much of a market for Web Developers since few people had heard of the Internet. So I read a few networking books and went into IT for a few years. I'd set up a start-up company's network, build them a website, and then move on to the next company. Later, I found myself working in online marketing and eCommerce website management. Though I've worked on many sites over the years, I miss the creativity and challenge of development. After deciding to upgrade my skillset, I visited several code bootcamps in Portland and chose Epicodus - even though it means two hours on the bus every day!
